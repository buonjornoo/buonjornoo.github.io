Activities

User Research

I’m used to do qualitative and quantitative user research across all stages of the design process. I plan and conduct interviews, usability tests, observational studies, and more. That way I provide important data that help to make informed decisions. Ultimately this helps to mitigate risks for the business.

User Experience Design

I use my insights to create all kinds of deliverables and outcomes such as wireframes, interface designs, design systems and developer handoffs.

Prototyping

Ideal should be tested as early as possible. Prototypes can take all kinds of forms: Paper, clickable presentations, interactive click dummys or even proof-of-concepts with Unity3d. Testing helps to figure out if we are on the right path or if we should go back to the drawing board.

Facilitating

Staying in aligned with the team and stakeholders is crucial. I plan and facilitate workshops in different stages during projects to make sure everyone is on the same page and we all own our project. This means kick-offs, ideations, empathy workshops after research, design sprints and retrospectives.

Approach

Understand

I start every project by trying to understand the problem, context and opportunities of the matter at hand. Depending on the knowns and unknowns I will plan and conduct fitting User Research activities to empathise with users, identify business needs and opportunities and gather technological constraints. Finally everything will converge into defining the problem we want to solve.
Empathise Define

Shape

Together we will find common ground in the problem space and align on a final problem definition. Now we will diverge again to ideate on possible solutions that adress the problem statement we agreed on. We will also decide which one (combination) of the ideas we want to persue first and build a prototype. This could be everything from a piece of paper or a simple software prototype that conveys our idea. , envision a desirable future state, prototype possible solutions, test them and iterate.
Ideate Prototype

Run

Now it’s time to run with our solution and expose it to real users. We wil observe how they use it and find out what we can do better. Sometimes it will bring us back to the drawing board, which is a good thing. Depending on what happens here, we will either go back and (re-) shape our solution or go further back to do more research to look at what we missed before.
Test

Principles

Outcomes over output

I value long term outcomes over short term output. I don’t create personas, journey maps and click dummies for the sake of creating output. My goal is to deliver value quickly and we will decide together how that outcome might look like. And yes, it might be personas, journey maps and click dummies.

Know your users

I get first hand information about the users, their needs, and goals. Only then I design for optimal user outcomes with your gathered insights.

Clarity

Don’t shy away from clomplexity. Embrace it when necessary but with a clear structure and hierarchy. Remove paradox choices and noise to make decisions as simple as possible. Respond to your users actions. Be consistent across all channels.

Embrace look and feel

Accessibility can be gorgeous but you will have to put a lot of effort into it. Let us go the extra mile to bring joy to all our users.
