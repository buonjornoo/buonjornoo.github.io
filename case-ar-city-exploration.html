AR City Exploration

An Augmented Reality application called Raubkunst.

For tech savvy tourists who want to get to know a city in a new way.

Interaction Design, storytelling, Unity3d prototyping, sound design, master thesis.

01 Summary

Finding a new approach for location based storytelling in AR.

We were able to create a holistic experience concept bringing together transmedia storytelling, interactive technology and real places. We also created a primising concept framework for explorative AR experiences and built an iOS prototype to showcase its application.

02 Discovery

It started with understanding the problem.

Design Thinking

From semantic analysis exercises over 10 plus 10s to ad hoc workshops we were our own facilitators throughout the whole process. We applied everything we learned in class in our autonomous project work. The Design Thinking methods helped us to design, ideate and make decisions throughout the whole project.

Research Wall

We used a physical research wall to align on our individual findings. This helped us to refer to specific things in discussions and remember why we made certain decisions earlier in the project.

Idea Portfolio and Feasibility Maps

We conducted mini workshops whenever we needed to solve design problems. We kept our idea portfolios on the Research Wall and later on digital, as you can see on the right. We also created personas, stakeholder maps, interaction tables and many more design artifacts.

Early and Regular Testings

We tried to use every opportunity to test our ideas before committing to them. We built paper prototypes to test our haptic experiences. We did a lot of white-boxing in Unity3D to test soundscapes and invited our university colleagues to give us feedback whenever possible. We also invited actors to act out our screenplays live on a stage to get a feeling of the mood in our story pieces.

End to End Experience Design

Experience design is about the whole process and all senses. We tried to make every design decision for Raubkunst as intentional as possible. We picked a starting place for our experience that has a lot of cafes around so tourists could not only buy themselves a beverage when needed but also enjoy the small of fresh coffee beans when they start our experience. Our app used vibration for haptic feedback and we kept our sound design at a level that blended into the environment. We kept the on screen interactions on a minimal level so tourists can enjoy looking around in the real world and our app would also remind our users to take a break and enjoy some snack on a local store nearby.

Holistic Design

Raubkunst aims to serve tech savvy tourists but there are tons of humans who could be affected by our product as well. Our concept also took other people in consideration by avoiding overly crowded places depending of the time during the day and tracking check-ins on other services like Google and Facebook. We also wanted to support local businesses by reminding our users to take a break when they are close to a local store. Even our haptic objects were planned to be shared with other potential users as an invitation to "join the game".

03 Solution

Our creations.

Experience Framework

We created a conceptual framework called Memorama to help producing experiences that are location based and bring together fiction, historical and cultural knowledge about the location and utilise contempo- rary technology to communicate information. This Memorama framework serves as a guideline for a coherent experience. Our Memorama framework consists of three abstract spaces: Navigation Spaces, Discovery Spaces and the Home Space. This separation is necessary to be understandable and consistent. We utilize playful orientation in Navigation Spaces, tell the engaging story in Discovery Spaces and wind down in the Home Space.

We used the 5-E framework to include all senses into our experience. It also helped us to make the end to end experience more elegant.

The 5E Experience Design Model helps to communicate an experience design concept. It is a framework for creating holistic, meaningful expe- riences and is an inclusive model which can add excitement, coherence and elegance to a service or an experience.

Andy Sontag in The 5E Experience Design Model

Application Design

We originally wanted Raubkunst to take place on wearable devices to fully immerse the user. But we decided to build our proof of concept on devices that are available to us now. Following examples from Pokemon Go and others, we used handheld devices. We limited ourselves to visual and sonic communication and aimed to reduce the time on screen to a minimum so users can fully experience the city.

Prototype

We created our proof of concept using Unity3d and Vuforia. I partnered with a fellow UX Developer in our team to create the scenes and test different kinds of functionalities like image trigger recognition, different story triggers and object positioning.
