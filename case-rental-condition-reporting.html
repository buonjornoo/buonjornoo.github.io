02 Discovery

It started with understanding the problem.

Kick Off Workshop

As externals we need to know everything the client already knows about the project, the problem they want to solve and how they think they are going to solve it. We facilitated a workshop to kick off this project as a team: Product Owner, Service Designer, UX Designers, Developer. We mapped the current status quo on a journey and identified our research focus. We aligned our expectations on what we must, should, could and wonâ€™t do during the project. Finally we solemnly agreed on how we are going to work together and treat each other.

App As A Hypothesis

Building an app was the goal of our client since the very beginning. We treated this app idea as a hypothesis that we would test. We focused our research on understanding the status quo to substantiate the business challenge at hand. Besides that we examined the assumed user groups and their goals in order to create personas we could design for. At the same time we needed to investigate the technological status quo of our clients branches in order to come up with a realistic solution.

We are currently not able to capture the status information and condition of an asset every time it changes the location.

Problemstatement

Interviews

We started our research with interviewing higher level employees to get a better understanding of the rental business, how it works and the high level processes behind it. We used our insights to bring more substance into our workshop journey map. The interviews also helped us to plan our semi structured user interviews later on.

Field Research

We needed to observe the process live at the branches to see how they do their job. The main reason behind it is that what users actually do is usually different than what they say they do. The second reason is that we needed so actually see the environments in which the solution would be used. How busy is it? How is the lighting? Is there wifi? Is there a network connection at all? How much space do they usually have?

Analysis

We brought back our research artifacts and physically clustered and mapped them in a room as a team. Before jumping into the solution space we would plan and facilitate an empathy and ideation workshop for the stakeholders that did not get to be a part of the research.

03 Solution

How we approached the solution space and what influenced us.

Workshop Ideation

After the empathy part to align on our research insights, we started ideating on solutions. We narrowed down the solution space to the app that was originally committed to by the sponsors of our product owner. But now we had enough substance to envision how that app would work, what it would do and who would use it.

Prototype Design

Directly after the workshop we transferred the manual sketches into the digital space where iterations could directly be manipulated and changes would be more easily documented. Our initial wireframes were ready in a matter of a few hours. It only took a little more effort to turn our wireframes into something clickable for our first tests.

Usability Testing

We tested remotely, on a tight schedule and only with a hand full of participants. The testing scope would be much larger as soon as this project would get commitment for development. But for this project scope we collectively decided to test in order to bring more substance into our click dummy.

Iterative Approach

We created four iterations of our click dummy based on user feedback. We changed the flow and how we would approach specific inputs but the core functionality of our solution got promising feedback.

04 Outcomes

More than just deliverables.

Deep Process Insights

Our research efforts payed off since we brought a lot of relevant insights together and made sense of it. Our client said that all the knowledge and data was there. It was our methods that helped to make use of it in a meaningful way.

Project Momentum

We effectively were able bring a lot of momentum to the project. Bringing together the sponsors and other stakeholders for an engaging interactive presentation was especially helpful to get everyone talking and committing to next steps.

Data Informed Personas

Our client and the product owner already had ideas about who would use the product. We brought substance to the possible users by using our research data to build personas. This helped us to figure out where to focus our energy on and will help us to empathize when we keep on adding relevant features to the product.

A Clear Path Forward

We helped the client to figure out what the next steps could look like. It wasnâ€™t only about who would build that product but also who would be able to maintain and scale it. We also helped to plan how to navigate their huge enterprise system, who to talk to, and which stakeholders to keep in mind.
